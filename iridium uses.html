How Iridium Is Used

Even though iridium is a very hard element, it is too brittle to be used alone. The way to use iridium is in an alloy (a mixture of iridium and another element). Iridium is used primarily to increase the strength and corrosion of the element platinum. Platinum-iridium alloys are used in the manufacturing industry for the production of machine parts, containers (e.g. crucibles), and electric contacts that may be exposed to high temperatures and/or chemicals that are corrosive. Jewlery and the electrodes of spark plugs can also be made from iridium alloys.
